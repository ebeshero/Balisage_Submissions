Balisage Paper: Correcting Collation Problems with XSLT

Untangling the Frankenstein Variorum

Elisa E. Beshero-Bondar

Professor of Digital Humanities

Program Chair of Digital Media, Arts, and Technology

Penn State Erie, The Behrend College

<eeb4@psu.edu>

Raffaele Viglianti

Research Associate

Maryland Institute for Technology in the Humanities (MITH) at the University of Maryland

Abstract

Computer-aided collation is like a power loom that inevitably tangles up threads in the machinery. Automating a tedious process magnifies the complexity of error-correction, calling for new tooling to help us smooth the weaving process. The authors are attempting to refine a collation algorithm to improve its alignment of variant passages in the Frankenstein Variorum project. In this paper we investigate how best to engage XSLT in the collation process, with our project files as a case study. We began with a Python script that tokenizes and normalizes the texts of the editions on spaces and delivers them to collateX for processing the collation and delivering TEI-conformant output for our project. Our pipeline has, to this point, applied XSLT to prepare the editions for collation and for post-processing following the collation to correct patterns of erroneous alignments. We are now experimenting with the Text Alignment Network's tandiff XSLT, introduced by Joel Kalvesmaki at the Balisage 2021 conference, to handle the string comparison completely with XPath and XSLT with a much different approach to the tokenization process. In this paper we discuss our experiments with collateX and tandiff. We seek to determine how far we can take XSLT in the preparation, collation, and correction process.
